The Lantern Trust · Weymouth, Portland and Dorset

Help, support and a safe place to be heard.

We support people facing homelessness, housing problems, financial hardship, mental health difficulties, addiction, isolation and crisis.

Nowhere safe to stay tonight?

If you are in immediate danger or at risk of violence, call 999. If you have nowhere to stay outside normal working hours, call Dorset Council’s emergency out-of-hours service on 01305 221000. During Lantern opening hours, contact us or visit the drop-in and we will help you understand your options.

What we help with

Practical help, emotional support and trusted relationships.

Services are grouped by need first, so people do not have to understand internal service names before asking for help.

Housing and homelessness

Housing support, homeless support, drop-in support and help understanding immediate options.

Money and benefits

Benefit advice, practical forms, appointments, money worries and signposting to specialist advice.

Mental health and emotional support

A calm space to be listened to, stabilise, build trust and work through what is happening.

Access Wellbeing and community

Support to reconnect, build confidence, reduce isolation and move forward at a safe pace.

How support works

People do not need to have everything worked out before they come to us.

1

Contact us or visit

People can call, email, use the referral route or visit the drop-in during opening hours.

2

We listen first

We take time to understand what is happening and what feels most urgent.

3

We agree the next step

This may be practical help, emotional support, advocacy, referral or connection to another service.

4

We stay alongside people where we can

Support is shaped around the person, their safety and the services available.

About Lantern

We listen first, then help people take the next step.

The Lantern Trust has supported people in Weymouth and Portland since 1983. We work with people facing complex challenges, offering practical support, emotional support and trusted relationships that help people move forward.

Stay safe online
